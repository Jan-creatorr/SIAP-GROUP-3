DIGITAL DIVIDE IN THE PHILIPPINES

16.2% of Filipinos remain offline in 2025. Internet access is uneven across cities and provinces, creating barriers in both learning and employment.

THE DIVIDE IN NUMBERS

• Urban areas average 90 Mbps internet speed
• Rural areas as low as 10 Mbps
• Digital economy = 8.5% of GDP (2024)

EDUCATION IN THE DIVIDE

• Students in remote areas miss online classes
• Teachers lack access to digital tools
• Families share a single device for learning

EMPLOYMENT IN THE DIVIDE

• BPO jobs are concentrated in cities
• Rural workers excluded from remote jobs
• Weak internet worsens inequality

WHAT IF?

What if BPO companies only hired workers from NCR due to poor internet in other provinces?

Rural unemployment would rise, widening inequality.

BRIDGING THE GAP

• Opens telco market to more competition
• Cheaper and faster internet nationwide
• Extends coverage to remote provinces
